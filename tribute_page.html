Ms. Neerja Bhanot

Here's a time line of Ms. Bhanot's life :

  • 1963 (September 7th): Neerja Bhanot is born in Chandigarh, India, to Harish and Rama Bhanot.
  • Early 1960s - 1970s: Neerja grows up in a loving family and attends Sacred Heart Senior Secondary School in Chandigarh.
  • Late 1970s: The Bhanot family relocates to Bombay (now Mumbai). Neerja continues her education at Bombay Scottish School.
  • Early 1980s: Neerja graduates from St. Xavier's College, Bombay.
  • 1980s: Neerja begins a modeling career and is recognized for her beauty and charm.
  • 1985: Neerja joins Pan Am World Airways as a flight attendant, fulfilling her desire to travel the world. She excels in her role and is known for her professionalism and dedication to passenger comfort.
    September 5th, 1986:
  • Pan Am Flight 73, with Neerja serving as senior flight purser, departs from Bombay en route to New York with a stopover in Karachi, Pakistan.
  • Shortly after landing in Karachi, four armed terrorists from the Abu Nidal Organization hijack the plane.
    17-hour Hijacking Ordeal (September 5th, 1986):
  • During the tense standoff, Neerja demonstrates exceptional bravery and composure.
  • She secretly hides the passports of American passengers, potentially saving them from targeted violence by the hijackers.
  • Neerja remains calm and supportive, reassuring frightened passengers while communicating with the hijackers.
  • When an opportunity arises, Neerja opens an emergency exit and helps passengers evacuate the aircraft.
  • Tragically, while assisting passengers, Neerja is shot and killed by the hijackers.
    September 5th, 1986 (Aftermath):
  • Pakistani commandos storm the aircraft and overpower the remaining hijackers.
  • News of Neerja's heroism reaches India and the world, sparking a global outpouring of grief and admiration.
    Post September 5th, 1986:
  • Neerja Bhanot is posthumously awarded the Ashoka Chakra, India's highest peacetime gallantry award, for her exceptional bravery and selflessness.
  • She is also awarded the Tamgha-e-Pakistan, a civilian honor from Pakistan, in recognition of her act of humanity towards Pakistani passengers.
  • Neerja's story continues to inspire countless individuals with her courage, selflessness, and unwavering commitment to helping others.
